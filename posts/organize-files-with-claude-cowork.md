Let's be honest most of us have a Downloads folder that looks like a digital junk drawer. Screenshots named `Screenshot 2024-11-03 at 4.12.22 PM.png`, random PDFs you downloaded once and never opened again, zip files from last year. Sound familiar?

In January 2026, Anthropic released **Claude Cowork**, an AI agent that can directly access your file system and organize things for you. Instead of just chatting about what _should_ be done, it can actually _do_ it: create folders, move files, rename things, and read the contents of documents to figure out where they belong. I've been using it for housekeeping tasks and it's saved me a lot of time.

---

## How to Set It Up

Here's how to get started:

1. Open [claude.ai](https://claude.ai), download the Desktop app on your Mac if you don't already have.
2. Look for the **Cowork** option (it's in the sidebar or settings depending on the version)
3. Grant it access to the folders you want it to work with
4. **Back up your files first**
5. Start with one folder at a time, not your entire hard drive
6. Give it clear, specific instructions and review what it does before moving on

The more specific you are with your instructions, the better. "Organize my Downloads folder" is fine to start. But "look through my Downloads, sort files by type, and flag anything that looks like a duplicate" will get you much better results.

## What I Used It For

### Pictures Folder

My Pictures folder was all over the place, a mix of old photos, random stuff, screenshots that ended up there somehow, and duplicates I'd accumulated without realizing it.

I asked Claude to go through the entire folder and:

- Move any duplicates or near-duplicates into a `To Delete/` folder for me to review
- Look at every remaining picture, rename it to something descriptive, and sort it into a category folder

It came back with folders like `Travel/`, `Family/`, `Of me/`, `Web References/`, etc. Each with properly named files. The `To Delete/` folder had a bunch of stuff I didn't need. I gave it one final look and deleted them.

### Screenshots Folder

I did the same thing with my Screenshots folder, which had years of unorganized screen captures. I asked Claude to look at each one, group them by what they were (app screenshots, receipts, design references, memes, code snippets), and delete anything that was clearly throwaway — loading screens, error messages I'd already resolved, that kind of thing.

### Documents Folder (PDFs)

For Documents, I focused on PDFs specifically. I asked Claude to open each one, read enough to understand what it was, and rename it to something useful while sorting into categories like `Invoices/`, `Resumes/`, `Taxes/`, `Project Assets/`, etc.

Feels like magic having AI look at PDF contents, understand and organize everything for you.

## Other Ideas Worth Trying

A few other uses I've read about onlne you can try:

- **Cleaning up a Downloads folder by file type** — sort everything into `Images/`, `Documents/`, `Videos/`, `Archives/` automatically
- **Organizing photos by date** — Claude can read EXIF metadata from photos and sort them into `2024/06/`, `2024/07/` folders
- **Turning a folder of receipt photos into an expense report** — extract vendor, date, and total from each receipt image, then output a spreadsheet with totals by category
- **Renaming a batch of "Untitled" files** — open each one, read the content, and suggest a real name
